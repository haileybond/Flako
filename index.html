McCallum + Flako

A history of the McCallum family and Flako Products Corporation of Highland Park, New Jersey

The Flako Products Corporation was founded in 1922 by Arthur McCallum in Highland Park, NJ and produced boxed pie crust and baking mixes for over 30 years, becoming a household name before the company sold to Quaker Oats in 1954.

The Original Crust

The famous Flako pie crust originated from an old McCallum family recipe. Arthur and his siblings grew up eating his mother's delicious pies. Arthur used his mother's crust recipe to develope the Flako pie crust over a period of three years, perfecting it for boxing and a long shelf life. The Founding of Flako

In September of 1921, Arthur McCallum founded the Flako Products Corporation in a small factory on Cedar Avenue in Highland Park, NJ. The company was officialy incorporated in January of 1922 and began producing boxed pie crust mix.

The boxed pie crust mix required only the addition of water and was a hit with busy housewives during the first half of the 21st century. By the 1940's, Flako Pie Crust grew to become a household name and the company was also producing boxed mixes for corn muffins and biscuits.

The McCallum Family

Arthur and Helen McCallum lived in Highland Park, NJ and founded the Flako Products Corporation in their hometown. Arthur served as president and Helen worked as secretary-treasurer.

The McCallums were very active members of the Highland Park borough, and in 1954 Arcadia Avenue was renamed McCallum Place in their honor.

Flako Products

In July of 1924, the company filed a trademark for the Fluffo logo. Fluffo was a prepared flour used to make biscuits or shortcakes, not to be confused with the Golden Fluffo shortening that was popular in the 1950's.

The Flako Products Corporation also produced a popular boxed corn muffin mix called Flakorn. The mix required only the addition of eggs and water, and simplified the home baking process for customers, claiming the muffins could be prepared and baked in 18 short minutes.

Flako Patents

On Jul 11, 1938 Arthur McCallum filed a patent for a bag-opening machine used in the Flako factory. The machine made the process of opening and filling bags more effient. The patent was approved as patent US2183081 A and was published Dec 12, 1939. View more patent information here. Arthur filed several other patents under the Flako Products Corporation, including a "Material Handeling Apparatus" and "Composite Package"

Quaker Oats

In 1955, The Flako Products Corporation was aquired by Quaker Oats, who continued to produce Flako products for many years.

Arthur and Helen McCallum

Arthur and Helen had no children, but had other family in Highland Park including Arthur's nephew, David Warren McCallum. David worked at the Flako Products Corporation for several years after returning from WWII where he served in the U.S. Airforce. David was married and had three children.

Arthur McCallum served as councelman for Highland Park, NJ from 1934 through 1939. Helen was a reporter for the Daily Home News before working for Flako. Arthur and Helen were instrumental in the founding of Highland Park's First Aid Squad. In 1949, the Flako Product Corporation donated a brand new Meteor Cadillac to be used as Highland Park's first ambulance. In 1954, Arthur and Helen were honored by the Highland Park Borough who renamed Arcadia Ave to McCallum Place in their honor. McCallum Place still exhists today, although Arthur and Helen's home in Highland Park is no longer standing.
